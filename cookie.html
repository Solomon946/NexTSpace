What Are Cookies?

The basics of browser cookie technology

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow the site to remember information about your visit — such as your login status, preferred language, or shopping cart contents — so you don't have to re-enter it every time you return.

Cookies are not executable programs and cannot carry viruses. They simply store key-value pairs of text that your browser sends back to the originating server on each subsequent request. Modern browsers give you complete control over which cookies to allow, block, or delete.

Session vs. Persistent Cookies
Session cookies are temporary — they're deleted when you close your browser tab. Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period (e.g., 30 days, 1 year) and are useful for remembering your preferences across visits.
🔒
First-Party vs. Third-Party
First-party cookies are set directly by NexTSpace and only readable by us. Third-party cookies are set by external services (like analytics providers) embedded in our pages, and are readable only by those services.

How We Use Cookies

The specific purposes behind our cookie usage

NexTSpace uses cookies for five primary purposes. We keep this list deliberately short — we only deploy cookies that serve a clear function and respect your privacy.

  • Authentication & Security — to keep you securely logged in, prevent CSRF attacks, and detect fraudulent sessions.
  • Preferences & Personalisation — to remember your theme preference (dark/light), language, region, and cart contents.
  • Performance & Analytics — to measure page load times, identify errors, and understand how visitors navigate the platform.
  • Marketing & Retargeting — only with your explicit consent, to show relevant ads and measure campaign effectiveness.
  • Feature Flags — to roll out new features gradually and conduct A/B tests without disrupting your experience.

We do not use cookies to build personal profiles for sale to advertisers, track you across unaffiliated websites without consent, or store sensitive information such as passwords or payment card details in cookies.

How We Share Cookie Data

Transparency on data flows from cookies

Cookie data — including identifiers, session tokens, and behavioural data derived from cookies — may be shared in the following strictly limited circumstances:

  • With analytics providers — pseudonymised aggregate data only, to improve our service.
  • With advertising platforms — only after explicit consent; used for measuring campaign ROI and retargeting.
  • For legal compliance — if required by applicable law, court order, or regulatory authority.
  • During business transfers — in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, with the same privacy commitments applying.

We never sell cookie-derived personal data to data brokers, advertisers, or any other third parties for their own commercial use. Aggregated, anonymised analytics data (which cannot identify you) may be used for industry benchmarking.

Do Not Track

Our response to browser Do Not Track signals

Some browsers transmit "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals to websites you visit. There is currently no agreed-upon industry standard for how websites should respond to these signals.

NexTSpace does not currently alter its data collection practices in response to DNT signals. We encourage you to use our built-in Preference Centre (Section 7) or browser cookie settings (Section 8) for granular control, which are more reliable than DNT signals.

We continue to monitor developments in the Global Privacy Control (GPC) standard and will implement support when a clear and legally recognised consensus emerges.

Legal Basis

The lawful grounds under GDPR and other regulations

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), UK, and other jurisdictions with specific cookie consent requirements, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Strictly Necessary / Legitimate Interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) — Essential cookies are deployed under the legitimate interest of providing a functioning service. No consent is required.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) — Analytics, marketing, and third-party embedded cookies require your freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent before activation.
  • PECR / ePrivacy Directive — For UK/EU users, we comply with the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations, which require prior consent for non-essential cookies.
  • CCPA (California) — California residents have the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information collected via cookies. See our Privacy Policy for full CCPA rights.
Right to Withdraw Consent
If you previously consented to optional cookies, you can withdraw that consent at any time using the toggles in Section 3 or the Preference Centre. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.
